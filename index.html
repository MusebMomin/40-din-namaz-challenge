About the Challenge

Understand the challenge rules, checkpoints, streak logic, and lifeline system.

What is the challenge?

  • This is a 40-day self-discipline challenge focused on consistent Salah.
  • Your goal is to keep your streak growing daily and reach the full 40-day path.
  • Every valid day moves your progress forward on the dashboard path.

What are checkpoints?

  • Checkpoints are milestone days that protect a portion of your progress.
  • If your streak breaks and no lifeline is available, your streak resets to the previous checkpoint.
  • In the visual challenge path, checkpoints are highlighted so you know your protected milestones.

When can streak break?

  • If any prayer is marked as Qaza.
  • If 2 or more prayers are marked No Jamat in a day.
  • If there is no submission for 2 consecutive days.

What is a lifeline?

  • A lifeline is a protection token that saves your streak before it breaks.
  • If a break condition happens and a lifeline is available, 1 lifeline is used automatically.
  • This keeps your streak alive and avoids resetting to the previous checkpoint.

How can a lifeline be earned?

  • Way 1: Complete all 5 prayers with Takbeer-e-Ula in a day → earn +1 lifeline.
  • Way 2: Reach streak milestone rewards:
  • • 10-day streak → +1 lifeline
  • • 20-day streak → +1 lifeline
  • • 30-day streak → +1 lifeline

Submission policy

  • You can submit only today or yesterday.
  • Only one submission per entry date is allowed.
  • For each prayer, choose exactly one status: Jamat, No Jamat, or Qaza.
